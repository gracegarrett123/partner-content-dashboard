            💡 Key Insights

            Modern Healthcare overtook Ad Age as the #1 brand — 14,353 visits (38% of portfolio) at a 2:33 dwell, ~28% above the 2:00 portfolio weighted average; its Cancer Immunotherapy podcast is the single biggest read (3,534). Move: lead health-system and pharma pitches with dwell, and commission a sponsored healthcare-audio slate off the podcast. Adobe

            Growth is reach, not engagement — portfolio +12.8% WoW (37,500 vs 33,237) while weighted dwell held flat at 2:00 (from 2:09). Move: do not sell the lift as engagement. Pair the new reach with premium-quadrant inventory so sponsors buy depth, not just volume. Adobe

            Ad Age is the dwell-at-scale proof point — YouTube Creators held 4:22 over 1,366 visits, ~120% above the 2:00 average. Move: package a creator-economy buy; the AI webcast has already converted (CMI Media went live today). Adobe Smartsheet

            40 of 123 measurable sponsored stories are premium inventory (high reach and high dwell, vs the 283 PV / 2:03 medians); 23 are hidden gems — proven content with broken distribution. Move: promote the 23 gems before commissioning new inventory; it is cheaper than making more. Tableau

            Concentration is the structural risk — MH + Ad Age are 74% of traffic; the six city brands plus Hart are ~20%. Move: build a city-brand vertical bundle (CRE / workforce / energy) and cross-sell the MH and Ad Age audience into it. Adobe

            ⚠️ Watch Areas

            Volume without engagement — verify before it reaches a sponsor deck. Cleveland's Centric Consulting AI-agents read drew 496 visits at a 0:09 dwell, dragging the brand average to 0:51. That is a bounce pattern, not a read. Move: confirm placement/tagging before it is reported as performance. Adobe

            Extreme dwell on tiny reach is noise, not insight. Bio/people pages are posting absurd averages (Cleveland "Kathleen Druzina" ~27 min on 8 pageviews). Caveat: avg time on page ≠ read time; treat sub-50-visit reads as directional and keep them out of client reporting. Tableau

            Measurement debt: we cannot see where half the audience comes from. "Unknown" is the largest arrival bucket at 46.8% of channel volume — bigger than Search (33.8%). The referrer simply is not captured. Move: fix UTM/referrer tagging; until then, make no channel-attribution claims in client decks. Tableau

            High dwell at scale — amplify these. Ad Age YouTube Creators (4:22 / 1,366), MH Expanding Lung Transplant (3:45 / 865) and MH Microbiome (2:59 / 1,126) all clear 3:00 at real volume. Move: feature on homepage + newsletter as sales proof. Hart Energy, meanwhile, fell to 312 visits (from 448) and Grand Rapids still leaks ~16% of visits (182/1,149) to a generic hub page. Adobe

            Biggest gap: the audience layer is still only half-built. We can now show arrival channel, section, user need, and subscriber/known/returning (48% of visits are returning; 930K subscriber visits) — but we still cannot show who engaged: company size, industry, named accounts, or intent, and we have no podcast downloads. Only 123 of 149 sponsored stories carry subscriber dwell. Move: wire in Libsyn + Bombora + Adobe firmographic reports, and make subscriber-time instrumentation a delivery requirement. Tableau

            Ideas & next moves — organized by lane. Each card: evidence → opportunity → the move, spanning new content, partnerships, products, marketing, and audience.

            📖 Metrics & caveats Adobe Tableau

            Visits / Page Views / Unique Visitors — from Adobe (site) and Tableau (sponsored, cumulative). Unique = distinct users in the window; page views exclude same-session repeats.

            Avg time on page — depth-of-engagement proxy. It is not listen/read time and (for podcasts) not download-based; individual listen data isn't available across platforms.

            Benchmark / pacing — actual vs the content-type benchmark (Campaign Performance master, current quarter) prorated to the window; where no benchmark exists we compare to the portfolio 2:09 average or prior week, and we name the comparator. ±15% = on pace.

            Not yet measured — audience firmographics (company size, industry, named accounts), intent (Bombora), traffic-source mix, and podcast downloads (Libsyn). These require new sources and are the highest-value add for client-grade insights.
