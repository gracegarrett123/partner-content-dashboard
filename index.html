            💡 Key Insights

            Modern Healthcare holds #1 at scale AND dwell — 14,624 visits (31% of the 13-brand portfolio) at a 2:35 avg, ~44% above the 1:48 portfolio weighted average; its Cancer Immunotherapy podcast is the single biggest read (3,296). Move: lead health-system and pharma pitches with dwell, and commission a sponsored healthcare-audio slate off the podcast. Adobe

            Automotive News debuts as the #3 brand — its first full week in the Adobe report lands at 5,044 visits, and its "European automotive network performance" read hit 2,338 (portfolio top 3). But dwell is thin (1:19 avg). Move: sell Auto News on reach and dealer-tech/mobility targeting, not engagement. Adobe

            The +22.7% "growth" is 4 new brands, not momentum — on the original 9 brands, traffic is up just 2.8% WoW (39,029 vs 37,968); Auto News, Plastics, Rubber and Tire added ~7,600 visits. Move: report the portfolio expansion and the organic number separately so the jump isn't oversold. Adobe

            Sponsored is 1.3% of pageviews but out-dwells editorial — 80K of 6.1M PV, yet sponsored median dwell (2:04) edges editorial (1:58). Volume isn't the sponsored story; attention is. Move: sell dwell and audience quality, not reach. Adobe

            Concentration is the structural risk — MH + Ad Age = 62% of traffic; add Auto News and the top 3 = 73%. The six city brands, Hart and the trade titles split the rest. Move: build a mid-tier reach bundle (Auto News + P&I + Chicago) and a city/trade vertical package to de-risk the dependence on two brands. Adobe

            ⚠️ Watch Areas

            Volume without engagement — verify before a sponsor deck. Cleveland's Centric Consulting AI-agents read drew 572 visits at a 0:16 dwell, dragging the brand to 1:22. A bounce pattern, not a read. Move: confirm placement/tagging before it's reported as performance. Adobe

            The four new trade brands are thin and low-dwell. Tire Business (281 visits / 0:55), Rubber News (462 / 1:15) and Plastics News (1,776 / 0:58) are real inventory but not heavy hitters. Move: package them as niche/vertical buys — don't slot them next to Ad Age/MH reach. Adobe

            Measurement debt: we can't see where ~half the audience comes from. "Unknown" is 46.8% of arrival volume — larger than Search. The referrer isn't captured. Move: fix UTM/referrer tagging; until then make no channel-attribution claims in client decks. Adobe

            High dwell at scale — amplify these. Ad Age YouTube Creators (4:47 / 1,410), MH UPMC Payvider (2:45 / 1,376) and the MH Cancer Immunotherapy podcast (2:16 / 3,296) clear strong dwell at real volume. Move: feature on homepage + newsletter as sales proof. Adobe

            Biggest gap: the audience layer is still half-built. We now show arrival channel, section, user need and subscriber/returning (47% returning; 1.04M subscriber visits) — but still not who engaged: company size, industry, named accounts, intent, or podcast downloads; and only 122 of 148 sponsored stories carry subscriber dwell. Move: wire in Libsyn + Bombora + Adobe firmographic/traffic-source reports. Adobe

            📖 Metrics & caveats Adobe Adobe

            Visits / Page Views / Unique Visitors — from the Adobe site report and the Adobe content extract (sponsored, windowed per pull). Unique = distinct users in the window; page views exclude same-session repeats.

            Avg time on page — depth-of-engagement proxy. It is not listen/read time and (for podcasts) not download-based; individual listen data isn't available across platforms.

            Benchmark / pacing — actual vs the content-type benchmark (Campaign Performance master, current quarter) prorated to the window; where no benchmark exists we compare to the portfolio 2:09 average or prior week, and we name the comparator. ±15% = on pace.

            Not yet measured — audience firmographics (company size, industry, named accounts), intent (Bombora), traffic-source mix, and podcast downloads (Libsyn). These require new sources and are the highest-value add for client-grade insights.
